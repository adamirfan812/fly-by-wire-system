What is Fly-By-Wire?

Fly-By-Wire (FBW) is a system that replaces traditional manual flight controls with an electronic interface. Pilot inputs are sent electronically to flight control computers, which process them and command the aircraft's control surfaces.

History of Fly-By-Wire

The Fly-By-Wire system originated in the 1950s and has evolved through time:

1950s-1960s

Early tests in military jets like the Avro Vulcan.

1970s

The Concorde was one of the first commercial aircraft with FBW.

1980s

Airbus A320 became the first commercial aircraft with a fully digital FBW system.

Present Day

FBW is the standard in modern aircraft such as the Boeing 787 and Airbus A350.

Key Components of a Fly-By-Wire System

Fly-By-Wire systems consist of various essential components, including:

  • Sensors: Collect data on the aircraft’s state, such as speed and altitude.
  • Flight Control Computers: Process pilot inputs and optimize control commands.
  • Actuators: Control the aircraft’s surfaces like the ailerons, elevators, and rudders.
  • Power Supply: Keeps the system operational.
  • Backup Systems: Redundant systems to ensure safety in case of failure.
